Portsmouth is England's most densely populated city

Portsmouth has 5145 people / km2

London has 4984 people / km2

Source: Nomis

The high population-density in Portsmouth results in vulnerable road users being concentrated into a small space

Vulnerable road users include
pedestrians, cyclists, motorcyclists and horseriders

Source: highway code

373 of Portsmouth's vulnerable road users became road casualties during 2012

One casualty for every day of the year
and a few spare
including:

  • 85 motorcyclists
  • 116 pedestrians
  • 172 cyclists

Road casualty figures only include those reported to the police, so these figures do not include all casualties from all incidents

Source: RRC2012

90 of Portsmouth's
373 road casualties in 2012 involved fatal or serious injury

That's more than 7 of us every month

One every four days

  • 29 motorcyclists
  • 35 pedestrians
  • 26 cyclists

Source: UK Gov Definitions

The UK Government defines
serious injury as:

  • Fractures
  • Concussion
  • Internal injuries
  • Crushings
  • Burns
  • Severe cuts
  • Shock requiring medical treatment
  • Anything requiring hospitalisation
  • Injuries causing death 30+ days later

Source: UK Gov Definitions
